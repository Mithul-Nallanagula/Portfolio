Chest And Triceps

Today’s workout is all about building a powerful upper body by focusing on your chest and triceps. Start with the classic bench press to target your pectoral muscles and increase overall chest strength. For the triceps, dive into tricep pushdown, an excellent compound movement for building mass. Finish with tricep pushdowns using a cable machine to isolate and sculpt your triceps. This chest and triceps routine not only enhances muscle definition but also boosts your pushing power, essential for upper-body strength.

Workouts:

1)Flat Dumbell Press

Dumbbell bench presses build muscle in your upper body. With proper form, the dumbbell bench press activates muscle groups throughout your upper body, including your triceps, pectoral muscles

2)Incline Dumbell press

The incline dumbbell press is a free weight exercise designed to target the chest, shoulders, and triceps, independently hitting each side of the body. This exercise is a great way to focus on and define your upper-chest muscles.

3)Cable Fly

The dumbbell chest fly is an exercise that can help to strengthen the chest and shoulders. It’s common to do it while lying on your back on a flat or incline bench. There’s also a dumbbell fly standing variation.

4)Triceps Pushdowm

The tricep pushdown uses a cable machine to train the tricep heads, with the medial and lateral heads mainly being targeted. The exercise involves pushing weight from around chest height down to the upper thigh. Both a straight bar and rope attachment can be used for this exercise. With free weights, the tension is often reduced during the eccentric part of the movement (when the weight goes with gravity).
